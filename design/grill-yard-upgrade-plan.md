# Grill Yard Upgrade Plan

## What The Thumbnail Has That The Current Level Is Missing

1. **A hero silhouette.** The tall covered quarter-pipe tower establishes the park immediately. The current park has many similarly weighted pieces and no dominant landmark.
2. **Connected lines.** The thumbnail aligns rails, banks, transitions, and landings so the eye can trace routes. The current modules read as isolated islands across a large slab.
3. **Useful density.** Foreground rails and ledges, a central funbox, a side bowl, and the tower overlap visually without blocking one another. Current empty floor makes every piece feel smaller.
4. **A strong edge landmark.** The burger shack, awning, patio, and roof burger occupy one side of the park and frame the action. The current shack interrupts the center lane.
5. **Layered scale cues.** Fence, planters, furniture, trees, houses, coping, bolts, and deck rails make the skate features feel large. The current perimeter jumps directly from concrete to distant foliage.
6. **Material hierarchy.** Cool poured concrete, honey plywood, yellow-painted steel, dark fence timber, and red-and-cream fabric create clear functional categories.

## Limited Modular Asset Kit

### Transition Kit

- Quarter pipe in 1.5 m, 2.5 m, and 3.5 m heights
- Spine/transfer made from paired quarter pipes
- Bowl using the shared transition profile
- Bank and roller pieces for approaches and return speed

### Street Platform Kit

- A-frame funbox with a flat deck and two bank approaches
- Manual pad in short and long lengths
- Stair platform with a center handrail
- Low concrete hubba/ledge

### Grind Kit

- Straight yellow round rail
- Down rail using the same posts and pipe
- Coping generated from the transition lip
- Deck guard rail for elevated silhouettes

### Social Prop Kit

- Striped shade canopy
- Picnic table and bench
- Concrete planter with low-poly shrubs
- Burger shack patio furniture

### Perimeter Kit

- Dark timber fence sections
- Small suburban house silhouettes
- Existing round trees and bushes
- Lamp posts and a few utility props

## Three Connected Lines

### A. Main Launch Line

Spawn pad -> pump roller -> A-frame -> center transfer -> covered hero quarter pipe -> broad landing lane. This is the readable first run and thumbnail camera axis.

### B. West Street Line

Entry bank -> manual pad -> flat rail -> stair/down-rail plaza -> return quarter. The return transition points back toward the A-frame so the line can loop.

### C. East Bowl Loop

Hip/pyramid -> integrated bowl -> low spine -> east quarter -> center transfer. This line is wider and more forgiving, with multiple transfer choices into the main line.

## Placement Rules

- Keep at least 7 m of clean run-up before major transitions.
- Keep 5 m clear beyond landings before the next forced obstacle.
- Place grind pieces parallel to travel unless they are deliberate transfer targets.
- Keep the burger shack and patio outside the three primary lanes.
- Use props to frame negative space, never to occupy landing zones.
- Place collectibles along complete routes rather than uniformly across the floor.

## Texture Set

- **Concrete:** cool light-gray poured concrete with subtle aggregate, faint patches, sparse cracks, and no directional lighting.
- **Plywood:** warm honey plywood with long grain, plank seams, and restrained screw heads, also without baked lighting.
- **Painted steel:** solid yellow material with highlights supplied by scene lighting.
- **Fence timber:** dark neutral brown to increase contrast around the playable deck.
